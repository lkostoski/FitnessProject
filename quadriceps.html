THE QUADRICEP MUSCLE

Quadriceps, refer to a group of four large muscles located at the front of the thigh. These muscles play a crucial role in various lower body movements, including walking, running, jumping, and stabilizing the knee joint. The four muscles that make up the quadriceps are the rectus femoris , vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, and vastus intermedius. Together, they contribute to the overall strength and function of the legs, supporting activities that involve extension of the knee and flexion of the hip joint.

Exersises that stimulate the quadriceps muscles

LUNGES WITH HIGH KNEE LIFT

    1. Perform a standard lunge by stepping forward with one leg.
    2. As you return to the starting position, lift the back knee toward your chest, engaging the rectus femoris
    3. Alternate legs for each repetition

SQUATS

  1. Position your feet wider than shoulder-width apart.
  2. Squat down by bending your knees and pushing your hips back.
  3. Ensure that your knees track over your toes and your weight is on your heels.
  4. Repeat

LEG PRESS

  1. Use a leg press machine and position your feet close together on the footplate.
  2. Press the weight away by extending your knees.
  3. Keep your feet flat and ensure that your knees stay aligned over your toes.
  4. Repeat
